## Article: Stop Guessing Your Working Weight. Use a 1RM Formula Stack Instead.

**Suggested tags:** Strength Training, Powerlifting, Fitness, Programming, Performance

---

Most lifters do one of two things:

1. Test a true max too often and burn out
2. Guess percentages from old numbers and miss progression windows

A better workflow is to estimate your one-rep max from your actual training sets, then program loads from that estimate.

That is exactly what [OneRepMaxx](https://onerepmaxx.com) is built for.

### Why a Formula Stack Beats a Single Formula

No 1RM equation is perfect in every rep range. A single formula can over- or under-estimate depending on reps, fatigue, and exercise variation.

The strongest practical approach is to calculate all three core formulas and use the consensus average:

- **Brzycki**
- **Epley**
- **Lombardi**

This reduces the bias of any one formula and gives a more stable anchor for programming.

### The Core Equations

For weight `w` and reps `r`:

- Brzycki: `w / (1.0278 - 0.0278 * r)`
- Epley: `w * (1 + r / 30)`
- Lombardi: `w * r^0.10`

In practice, your consensus estimate is:

```
consensus = (brzycki + epley + lombardi) / 3
```

### Practical Rule: Use 3-5 Rep Inputs for Best Signal

If your goal is high-confidence load planning, use recent hard sets in the 3-5 rep range. That range usually gives cleaner estimates than very high-rep sets where fatigue and pacing distort output.

You do not need to max out to get useful numbers.

### Program Loads From Percentages, Not Emotion

Once you have a current estimate, percentage work becomes straightforward:

- ~90%: heavy strength focus
- ~80%: classic volume-strength bridge
- ~70%: higher-volume work capacity

OneRepMaxx automatically maps your estimated max into:

- **Training zones table**
- **Program presets**
- **Warm-up ramp**
- **Competition attempt suggestions**

That turns one top set into a full training-day framework.

### Warm-Up Planning Without Guesswork

Most lifters under-warm or over-fatigue before working sets.

A progressive ramp based on your current estimated max gives enough neural readiness without wasting reps:

- Empty bar prep
- Mid-range activation sets
- One or two near-working-weight singles

The warm-up tab in [OneRepMaxx](https://onerepmaxx.com) gives a clean sequence and rounded plate-ready loads.

### Meet Day and Testing Day Attempt Strategy

A common failure pattern is opening too heavy.

A safer structure:

- Opener around ~90%
- Second around ~97%
- Third around ~103% (if speed and confidence are there)

Attempt selection should be boring early and aggressive late. The competition tab helps enforce that logic quickly.

### Track Progress Like a System, Not a Memory Game

Good programming depends on trend visibility:

- Is your estimate rising month-to-month?
- Which lift is stalling?
- Are you adapting or just accumulating fatigue?

### Bottom Line

You do not need to test maxes every week.
You need reliable estimates, consistent percentages, and progression you can see.
